Lagrange Multiplication¶

$$ min f(x) $$

constraint to $$ h(x) = 0 g(x) <= 0 $$

Where Karush Kuhn-Tucker Conditions is: $$ L = (x,\lambda) = f(x) - \lambda g(x)$$ Where the $x^*$ is a minimum and regular point respect to the constraint $ \exists \lambda \in {\rm I\!R} $, $ \mu \in {\rm I\!R}$, where $\mu >= 0$ and $$\nabla (x^*) + \lambda ^T \times \nabla(x^*) + \mu^T \times \nabla g(x^*) = 0$$ $$ \mu^T \times g(x^*) = 0 $$

where that is the final version of the first order necessary conditions of a problem which has both equality or inequality constraint

KKT necessary conditions¶

$$ f(x)...(1) $$$$ g(x) - b_i >= 0, i=1,...,k ...(2) $$$$ g(x) - b_i = 0, i=1,...,k ...(3) $$
Description Equation Applies To
Feasibility $$ g_i(x^*)-b_i $$ 2,3
No direction which improves objective and is feasible $$ \nabla f(x^*) - \sum_{i=1}^m \lambda_i^* g_i(x^*) = 0 $$ 1,2,3
Complementary slackness $$\lambda^*(g_i(x^*)-b_i)=0, i=1,...,k$$ 2
Positive Lagrange multipliers $$\lambda_i^* >=0, i=1,...,k $$ 2

Problem Defined¶

given
$$ f(x) = (x_1-8)^2 + (x_2-8)^2 $$ constraint to
$$ g_1(x) = x_1 + x_2 <= 12 $$ $$ g_2(x) = x_1 <= 6 $$ $$ g_3(x) = x_1,x_2>=0 $$ check if is feasible $$ g_i(x^*)-b_i $$ $$x_1 + x_2 -12 <=0 $$ $$x_1 - 6 <= 0 $$

$$ \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_1} \\ \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_2} \\ \end{bmatrix} - \lambda_1 \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial g_1}{\partial x_1} \\ \frac{\partial g_1}{\partial x_2} \\ \end{bmatrix} - \lambda_2 \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial g_2}{\partial x_1} \\ \frac{\partial g_2}{\partial x_2} \\ \end{bmatrix} = 0 $$
